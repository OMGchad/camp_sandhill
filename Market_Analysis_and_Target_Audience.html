Target Customer Segments

1. Youth Sports Groups (Ages 8-18)

Primary Sports Focus:

  • Running
  • Basketball
  • Football
  • Soccer
  • Swimming
  • Rowing
  • Tennis
  • Pickleball

Characteristics:

  • Local school groups
  • Seasonal camp programs
  • Team-building and skill development
  • Affordable accommodation needs

Pricing Strategy:

  • Dormitory rooms: $60-100/night
  • Cottage bunks: $20-35/person
  • Tent camping: $20-35/night

2. Corporate Retreats (Teams of 50-200)

Target Companies:

  • Local businesses (WESH 2 News, AdventHealth, Track Shack)
  • Regional corporations
  • Team-building and strategic planning sessions

Value Proposition:

  • Unique outdoor setting vs. traditional hotel conference rooms
  • Team-building activities and amenities
  • Flexible meeting spaces
  • Professional accommodations with camp-themed experiences

3. Wedding Market (5-200 guests)

Venue Options:

  • Large outdoor venue (200+ capacity)
  • Indoor venue for weather backup
  • Small intimate outdoor setting
  • Free wedding chapel concept (similar to Chapel Dulcinea, Austin, TX)

Pricing Strategy:

  • Premium venue rental fees
  • Turn-key wedding packages
  • Catering and event management services

4. Micro-Conventions

Target Communities:

  • Trading card conventions
  • Niche hobby groups
  • Local business associations
  • Special interest communities

Revenue Model:

  • Title sponsorship fees (~$20,000)
  • Venue rental
  • Food and beverage sales
  • Merchandise and ticket sales
  • Hotel/dormitory bookings

5. Public Events

Activities:

  • Running trails and run clubs
  • Brewery events
  • Farmers markets
  • Community meetups
  • Public park areas with volleyball courts and playgrounds

Competitive Analysis

Direct Competitors

1. Wekiva Youth Camp

  • Strengths: Established youth camp program
  • Weaknesses: Limited amenities, seasonal operation
  • Differentiation: Our comprehensive amenities and year-round operation

2. Circle F Dude Ranch

  • Strengths: Similar activity catalog, rental capability
  • Weaknesses: Equestrian focus, limited accommodation variety
  • Differentiation: Our multi-sport focus and accommodation flexibility

3. Camp Shalom of Central Florida

  • Strengths: Religious camp market
  • Weaknesses: Limited location, specific demographic
  • Differentiation: Our secular, multi-demographic approach

4. Evermore Orlando Resort

  • Strengths: High-end market, corporate accommodations
  • Weaknesses: No camping options, limited accessibility
  • Differentiation: Our economic accessibility and camping options

Market Gap Analysis

No comparable facility exists in Orlando market that offers:

  • Multi-level accommodations (tent to luxury)
  • Comprehensive sports and recreational amenities
  • Year-round event capabilities
  • Corporate and youth group flexibility
  • Signature 5K trail feature
  • Microbrewery integration

Market Demand Assumptions

Seasonal Patterns

  • Summer (June-August): School camps and youth programs
  • Spring/Fall/Winter: Corporate events, weddings, micro-conventions
  • Year-Round: Public events, run clubs, brewery operations

Occupancy Expectations

  • Target: Fully booked year-round with minimal slow seasons
  • Peak Capacity: 500+ guests
  • Revenue Optimization: Multiple simultaneous events through facility design

Market Entry Strategy

Phase 1: Foundation

  1. Land Acquisition: 150-250 acres in Orlando area
  2. Core Development: Essential facilities and infrastructure
  3. Initial Partnerships: Local schools and businesses
  4. Soft Launch: Limited events and camps

Phase 2: Growth

  1. Full Operations: Year-round programming
  2. Partnership Expansion: Corporate contracts and sponsorships
  3. Amenity Enhancement: Additional recreational features
  4. Market Penetration: Regional expansion of services

Phase 3: Optimization

  1. Revenue Diversification: Multiple income streams
  2. Operational Excellence: Streamlined processes
  3. Market Leadership: Premier destination status
  4. Expansion Opportunities: Additional locations or services

Risk Factors

Market Risks

  • Economic Downturn: Reduced corporate spending on retreats
  • Competition: New entrants in the market
  • Seasonal Fluctuations: Weather impact on outdoor activities

Mitigation Strategies

  • Diversified Revenue: Multiple customer segments and services
  • Strong Partnerships: Long-term contracts and relationships
  • Flexible Facilities: Indoor/outdoor options for all seasons
  • Community Integration: Local business support and partnerships
