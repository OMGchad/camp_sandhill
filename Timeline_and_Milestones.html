Critical Path Analysis

Critical Dependencies

  1. Funding: All development depends on securing adequate funding
  2. Land Acquisition: Construction cannot begin without property ownership
  3. Permits: Construction requires all necessary permits and approvals
  4. Design: Construction requires completed architectural and engineering plans
  5. Contractors: Construction requires qualified and available contractors

Risk Mitigation

  • Funding Risk: Multiple funding sources and backup plans
  • Land Risk: Multiple site options and contingency plans
  • Permit Risk: Early permit applications and professional assistance
  • Construction Risk: Qualified contractors and project management
  • Timeline Risk: Buffer time and flexible scheduling

Contingency Planning

Timeline Delays

  • Funding Delays: Alternative funding sources
  • Permit Delays: Professional permit expediting
  • Construction Delays: Additional contractors and resources
  • Weather Delays: Indoor construction priorities

Budget Overruns

  • Cost Controls: Regular budget reviews and adjustments
  • Value Engineering: Cost-saving design modifications
  • Phased Development: Prioritize essential facilities
  • Alternative Funding: Additional funding sources if needed

Market Changes

  • Demand Shifts: Flexible facility design
  • Competition: Unique value proposition maintenance
  • Economic Changes: Conservative financial planning
  • Regulatory Changes: Compliance monitoring and adaptation
